Welcome to the Face Morph Perception Study

You’ll be involved The study takes ~25 minutes.

What you’ll do

  • Brief pre-test to check your display and controls and to make sure you understand the task.
  • In the first stage, you have to complete a sequence of facial expression matching tasks.
  • In each matching task, mark your just noticeable difference (JND) points, i.e., the leftmost point on the slider having the same expression as the reference face.

Tips

  • Use a laptop/desktop at 100% zoom.
  • Dim bright ambient light; sit ~50–70cm from the screen.
  • Keyboard: ←/→ to adjust, Enter to confirm (also clickable).

Consent

This is a voluntary research study at Georgia Tech. We are studying how people perceive facial expressions, and how this perception may differ across individuals. In this task, you will view pairs of facial images and make pairwise comparison judgments (i.e., whether two expressions are the same or different), and you may also adjust facial expressions using a slider. There are no right or wrong answers—we are interested in your perception.

The study takes about 12 minutes to complete. First, you will answer some short demo questions to ensure you understand the task. Then you will proceed to the main task. When you finish, you will receive $2 as payment.

The risks are no greater than other tasks on Prolific, and you may not get any personal benefit. The study does not collect any identifiable information, except your anonymized Prolific ID for payment. Anonymous data may be made public to other researchers in future.

The Georgia Tech IRB and the Office of Human Research Protections may review study records. We will comply with any applicable laws and regulations regarding confidentiality. If you have questions, you can contact Ashwin Pananjady (ashwinpm@gatech.edu). If you have questions about your rights as a research subject, you can contact Georgia Tech IRB (IRB@gatech.edu).
